ROLE OF THE HOUSE

The House of Representatives is one of the two houses in the United States Congress, the legislative branch of the American government. Their power is established in Article One of the U.S. Constitution, which allows the house to pass federal legislation, although it must also be approved by the Senate and the President before it officially becomes a law, as well as impeach federal officials for treason, bribery, or other high crimes and misdemeanors. The House has committees and subcommittees to review bills and oversee the executive branch. The entire House makes the appointment of committee members, but the choice of members is made by their respective political parties. Each state is allowed to elect a certain number of representatives that is proportional to the state’s population, and each state is entitled to at least one. Representatives serve two-year terms. The Senate currently holds a Republican majority and Democratic minority with 236 Republicans and 193 Democrats.

LEADERSHIP IN THE HOUSE

The current Speaker of the House is Paul Ryan. The speaker is elected by the House of Representatives and acts as its leader through the combined roles of presiding officer and administrative head of the House, leader of the majority party in the House, and the representative role as an elected member of the House. The Speaker of the House is second-in-line to succeed the President, after the Vice President. The Majority and Minority Leaders are Kevin McCarthy (R-CA) and Nancy Pelosi (D-CA) respectively. Their positions are similar to that of the Senate, where they are the spokespeople and representatives for their respective parties, as well as advocates for their party’s objectives and agenda. They are voted on by the House of Representatives. The Majority and Minority Whips are Steve Scalise (R-LA) and Steny Hoyer (D-MD) respectively. They gather votes from their party members on major issues and keep track of the attendance of the legislature when representatives vote. They are also voted on by their party.

WORK DONE BY THE 115th CONGRESS:

On Health Care:
-- Repealed the Affordable Care Act
-- Passed the American Health Care Act

On Abortion:
-- Made the Hyde Amendment into a permanent law
-- Banned abortion after 20 weeks of pregnancy

On the Internet:
-- Repealed internet data privacy
-- Attempted to restore net neutrality (after the FCC repealed it)

On Foreign Relations:
-- Reaffirmed commitment to NATO
-- Declared North Korea a state sponsor of terror
-- Imposed sanctions on North Korea, Iran, and Russia

On the Military:
-- Authorized fiscal funding for the military and defense department

On Taxes:
-- Passed tax reform legislation

On the Environment:
-- Eased emission regulations on coal and power plants

On the Wellbeing of Citizens:
-- Provided disaster relief and tax breaks for hurricane victims
-- Passed bills addressing the opioid epidemic (such as substance abuse programs)

On Confirmations:
-- Confirmed the entirety of Trump’s Cabinet
