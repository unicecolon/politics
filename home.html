MIDTERM ELECTIONS

Congressional midterm elections take place between presidential elections. State and local races happen every year. Your state's representation in Congress (both the Senate and the House of Representatives) is affected by the midterm elections. Unlike the presidential elections, the electoral college is not employed; congressional elections use the direct number of votes by the state's citizens to determine who is elected. These elections are incredibly important, as they determine whether the Democratic or Republican party will hold the majority in each chamber of Congress for the next four years.
